Service Overview

Navigating Tanzania's Customs Landscape

Customs clearance is the most critical and complex stage of any international shipment entering or leaving Tanzania. A single error in classification, documentation, or compliance can result in costly delays, penalties, or cargo seizure. Sachsen Spedition's licensed customs brokers eliminate this risk.

We operate within the Tanzania Revenue Authority's (TRA) TANCIS electronic customs management system, processing declarations with precision and speed. Our team stays current on every tariff update, regulatory change, and procedural amendment issued by TRA, the Tanzania Bureau of Standards (TBS), and other regulatory bodies.

From initial tariff classification to final duty payment and cargo release, we manage the entire customs process. Our goal is simple: clear your goods at the lowest lawful duty rate, in the shortest possible time, with zero compliance issues.

Regulatory Expertise

TRA Customs Process

Understanding the Tanzania Revenue Authority's customs framework and how we navigate it on your behalf.

1

Pre-Arrival Declaration

We submit your customs declaration through the TANCIS system before your cargo arrives, enabling pre-assessment and faster clearance upon landing or docking.

2

Assessment & Duty Payment

TRA assesses duties and taxes based on our classification. We verify the assessment for accuracy, arrange payment through authorized banking channels, and obtain release authorization.

3

Inspection & Verification

We coordinate with TRA inspection teams, TBS quality inspectors, and any other regulatory bodies that need to verify your cargo. Our agents are present for every physical examination.

4

Release & Delivery

Upon clearance, we obtain the release order and coordinate immediate collection of your goods from the port or airport for transport to your designated premises.

Our Network

Regulatory Bodies We Work With

TRA

Tanzania Revenue Authority — primary customs authority responsible for all import/export duties and tax collection.

TBS

Tanzania Bureau of Standards — quality control and standards compliance for imported goods entering the Tanzanian market.

TFDA

Tanzania Food and Drugs Authority — regulation of food products, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and medical devices.

TPA

Tanzania Ports Authority — port operations, berth allocation, and cargo handling at the Port of Dar es Salaam.

TPRI

Tropical Pesticides Research Institute — permits for agricultural chemicals, pesticides, and related products.

NEMC

National Environment Management Council — environmental compliance for restricted or controlled substances and materials.
